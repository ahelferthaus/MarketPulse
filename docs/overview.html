The product
One number for market psychology

MarketPulse distills the market's emotional state into a single daily score from 0 (extreme risk-off) to 100 (extreme risk-on), presented on a five-zone barometer. It answers the question every client asks first — "how nervous is the market right now?" — with a measured, repeatable, explainable reading.

0–100daily composite score, five named zones
4markets scored independently: S&P 500, Nasdaq 100, Russell 2000, Dow
23 yrsof point-in-time history, 2003 to today
3lenses: market data, news narrative, positioning

The score is built from three families of evidence, echoing how an investment committee actually reads sentiment:

  • Classic — what the market is doing: price momentum, volatility, credit spreads, breadth. The hard data of risk appetite.
  • Narrative — what the market is saying: sentiment scored from financial news text. This lens accrues live and is clearly labeled while its history builds.
  • Positioning — where money is leaning: options activity, volatility term structure, and safe-haven flows.

Every reading ships with its regime label, direction, a data-confidence score, an honest as-of timestamp, and a plain-English explanation of what moved. The product is deliberately transparent: a public methodology page documents every component.

Why the vocabulary matters. MarketPulse deliberately avoids the "fear and greed" framing of the well-known CNN index. The zones speak the language of institutional risk — Panic, Defensive, Neutral, Risk-On, Euphoria — which is more precise, more professional, and ownable as Westwood intellectual property.

How it reads
Five zones, drawn from evidence

Zone boundaries are not arbitrary quintiles — they are asymmetric bands derived from the historical distribution of market conditions, so each zone means something about frequency as well as level.

Nine components feed the composite. Four are live today with twenty-three years of point-in-time history — momentum (price vs. 125-day average), volatility (VIX percentile), credit spreads (high-yield OAS), and safe-haven demand (equities vs. Treasuries). The remaining components — put/call ratios, breadth, new highs/lows, flows, and news narrative — are staged on the roadmap, each added with the same no-look-ahead discipline.

At extremes the barometer is a contrarian instrument: the research literature and our own backtest agree that deep Panic readings have historically preceded above-average forward returns, while Euphoria compresses them. That framing — discipline against the crowd — is the Westwood voice.

The record
Twenty-three years of the barometer

MarketPulse is not a black box launched yesterday. The engine was walked forward through every trading day since 2003 — each day scored using only the data available on that day — producing a genuine point-in-time record across every modern market regime.

S&P 500 MarketPulse composite, 2003–2026 (5-day average), with the five zones shaded. Every major stress episode registers as a plunge into Panic; every extended bull phase presses into Euphoria.
  • The global financial crisis, the 2011 downgrade, the Q4 2018 selloff, COVID, the 2022 bear market, and the spring 2025 stress all print as deep Panic readings — the instrument sees what investors felt.
  • History is reconstructed from the four live components (momentum, volatility, credit, safe haven); the narrative lens accrues from launch forward and is excluded from the historical composite — stated plainly wherever the history is shown.
  • The same walk-forward engine refreshes the site daily, so the public record and the research record can never diverge.

Under the hood
Built like research, run like software
  • Point-in-time discipline. Every historical value uses only data available on that date; rolling windows are strictly trailing. The backtest engine that validates regimes enforces the same rule — no look-ahead, anywhere.
  • Tiered data sourcing. Public tier (exchange data, FRED credit spreads) powers the deep history at zero data cost; the professional tier — Westwood's Bloomberg infrastructure — verifies the most recent sessions whenever a Terminal is present. Raw licensed data never leaves the building: only derived 0–100 scores are published.
  • Honest by construction. The public page shows when data is a static snapshot vs. live, the exact as-of time, how many sources are reporting, and a confidence score computed from data quality — not marketing copy.
  • Modern, owned stack. Python analytics engine and API, analytical database, React front end; deploys as a static site (near-zero hosting cost) or a live API. Fully Westwood-built and Westwood-owned.
  • Embeddable. A widget endpoint already exists — the barometer can sit on westwood.com, in a partner's page, or inside advisor collateral with one line of HTML.
  • Daily automation. Scores recompute and republish automatically after each US close; the historical record and the live site can never drift apart.
Compliance posture
Educational by design
  • MarketPulse is an educational market-sentiment indicator — explicitly not investment advice, not a trading signal, and labeled as such on every surface.
  • No performance promotion, no product recommendations, no client data — the cleanest possible footprint under the SEC Marketing Rule, pending formal review.
  • Sources are public or properly licensed; published values are derived scores, never redistributed raw data.
